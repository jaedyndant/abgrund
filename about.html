ABOUT
01 MANIFESTO

We built this festival because the ones we wanted to attend stopped existing.

ABGRUND started in 2023 in a rented warehouse in Leipzig-Plagwitz with a borrowed projector, a PA from a friend's studio, and seventy people who were tired of festivals that felt like branded content.

The name means "abyss" in German. We liked the honesty of it. Most experimental work happens at edges, in depths, in places that aren't comfortable or well-lit. We wanted a festival that felt like that.

02 PRINCIPLES

No sponsors. No phones in screenings. No overlap on headliners.

We don't take brand money. The festival is funded by ticket sales, a small arts council grant, and volunteer labour. This means we stay small, which is the point.

Phones are locked in pouches during film screenings. We're not precious about it elsewhere, but if you're in a dark room watching 16mm film, the screen in your pocket stays there.

No two headline acts play at the same time. You don't come to ABGRUND to make choices between things you want to see. You come to see everything.

03 VENUES

Three spaces. One district. No shuttle buses.

Spinnerei — Former cotton mill turned art complex. Hall A for feature films and the opening program. Hall B for short films. Studio spaces for workshops. Capacity: 400.

Westwerk — Live music venue. Main room for headline performances. Basement for late-night DJ sets. Capacity: 600 main / 200 basement.

Kunstkraftwerk — Former power station. Sound installations in the turbine hall. Immersive A/V performances. Ryoji Ikeda's closing set happens here. Capacity: 800.
